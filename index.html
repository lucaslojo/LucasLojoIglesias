About

With an interdisciplinary background in engineering, social sciences, and –long ago– musical studies, I have an interest in studying socio-technical systems, policy design, and initiatives aimed at advancing humanistic innovation –which I look forward to explore in current and future projects in and out of Academia–. Recent projects and roles have included work at Planetary Health Biosolutions in the biotech sector, the metabolic engineering field, climate investments management, and exploring the power of food fermentation at Noma, DTU, Chr. Hansen, and Fermentation Corp., as well as initiatives in urban design. In the meantime, I have developed an interest in exploring the interface between science, engineering, and the arts—including an old passion for painting and visual imagery. Currently I am developing, together with colleagues, a platform to share academic and PhD-level economic research with a broader audience. Other previous work and activism have included collaborations with NGOs in Spain and Denmark, or Wikileaks LatinAmerica-Spain research, including initiatives affecting distinctive administrations in Euroasia.
